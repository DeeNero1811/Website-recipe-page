Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 25 minutes

Cooking Time: 1 hour

Servings: 5-6

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 derica of Rice
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) vegetable oil
  • 1 meduim (8- ounce:225g) red onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 dried bay leaves and 2 teaspoon of dried tyhme
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 475g plum tomatoes
  • 1/4 of a Scotch bonnet pepper, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon paste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon paste

Instructions

  1. Combine tomato, red bell pepper, garlic, ginger, and water in a blender for the sauce. Blend on high until smooth and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and salt and stair until onion starts to soften, 4 to 5 minutes. Clear space in the middle of the pan, add tomato paste, and stair for 2 to 3 minutes. Season with paprika, curry powder, thyme, pepper, and turmeric; continue cooking for 1 to 2 minutes until everything is well combined.
  3. Turn off the heat, and stir in the rice, making sure every grain of rice is coated with the oil-tomato mixture. Add bay leaf and chicken bouillon paste.
  4. Turn heat to high, stir in tomato sauce, and bring to a vigorous simmer. Cover with a tight fitting lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for 20 minutes. Do not remove the lid or stir rice.
  5. Turn off heat after 20 minutes, and set the timer for 12 minutes. Allow rice to sit and do not remove the lid.
  6. After the 12 minutes are up, remove lid, and fluff rice with a pastula.
  7. If rice seems to be a little undercooked at this point, stop fluffing. add a little bit of water or the water you used in cooking your turkey, cover tightly again, and set heat to medium low. Cook for another 5 minutes, and check again.

Tips & Notes

Tip: Let the sauce simmer longer for deeper flavor. You can also use a mix of beef and pork for a richer taste.
